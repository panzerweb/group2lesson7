Filipino Women in the Workforce

Published on February 21, 2025

Filipino women have joined the labor force due to economic necessity and opportunities. Despite growth in numbers and participation, women still face challenges in achieving employment parity with men.

Labor force participation remains lower than men's rates. Family responsibilities often constrain employment opportunities.

What is the Government's Goal?

Date Published: Apr 02, 2023

According to National Economic and Development Authority (NEDA), the goal of the government is to increase women participation in the labor force by creating more opportunities available.

Moreover, the Government's target under Philippine Development Plan or PDP from 2023 to 2028 is for female force participation rate to rise in between 52 and 54 percent in 2028.

What are the Factors?

Based on NEDA's study from 2019, marriage and childbearing are among the most associated towards a heavy decline in the female labor force participation. As those under the factors are 40% less likely to participate in the labor market than those single status individuals.

The other factor is of gender stereotype that are still a trend such as domestic-related roles while their male counterparts participate in the labor force. As well as Gender wage gap where according to Philippine Institute for Development Studies that women in digital jobs earn 18.4% less then men.

What can they do?

Among the strategies of Philippine Development Plan is to uptrend gender and green competencies to address gender bias and gender role stereotypes in the field of entrepreneurship and opportunities. Moreover, Edillon said to provide lifelong learning especially digital technology for women.

Gender and Green Competencies:

  • Reduce gender bias and stereotypes that limit women's economic opportunities.
  • Equip individuals with skills to participate in sustainable and green industries.
  • Encourage businesses and startups to adopt gender-inclusive and environmentally friendly practices.
